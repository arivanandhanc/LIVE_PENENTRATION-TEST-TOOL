Privacy Policy

How Arivanandhan SecTools handles the data involved in a scan.

Last updated:

1. Who we are

Arivanandhan SecTools ("the Service", "we") is a web-application security-testing tool operated by Arivanandhan Chitheshwaran at sectools.arivanandhan.in. This policy explains what data the Service processes when you run a scan.

2. Data you provide

  • Target details — the URL/hostname you ask us to scan.
  • Scan configuration — the chosen profile and modules.
  • Optional credentials — if you choose an authenticated scan, any cookies, bearer token, or headers you supply. These are used only to send authenticated requests to your target during that scan.
  • Optional manifests — a package.json/lockfile you paste for the dependency audit.

3. Data we generate

Running a scan produces a scan record: the discovered attack surface, the findings, an activity log, and the generated report. By default these are stored as JSON files on the server hosting the Service so you can revisit the result and export the report.

4. How credentials are handled

Authentication material you supply for an authenticated scan is held in memory for the duration of the scan and used solely to authenticate requests to the target you specified. Treat any session you paste as disposable — revoke/rotate it after testing. Do not paste long-lived production credentials.

5. Outbound requests

To perform a scan, the Service makes HTTP(S) requests to the target you specify and may query public threat-intelligence sources on your behalf, including the OSV vulnerability database (for the dependency audit) and Certificate Transparency logs via crt.sh (for subdomain enumeration). Only the data necessary for those lookups (e.g. package names/versions, the apex domain) is sent.

6. Retention & deletion

Scan records persist on the host until deleted. If you self-host, you control retention entirely — scan JSON lives in the data/ directory and can be removed at any time. On the hosted service, contact us to request deletion of a specific scan record.

7. What we do not do

  • We do not sell or share your scan data with third parties for marketing.
  • We do not use supplied credentials for any purpose beyond the requested scan.
  • We do not scan targets you have not explicitly submitted.

8. Your responsibilities

You must only scan systems you own or are explicitly authorised to test. See the Acceptable Use policy.

Note: This policy is provided for transparency about the tool's data handling and is not legal advice. If you operate this Service publicly, have it reviewed against the privacy laws applicable to you and your users.
